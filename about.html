About Henotes Data Dossier

Building Africa’s data-oriented workforce

Who We Are

Hennotes Data Dossier (HDD) is a data capability and analytics enablement company focused on helping organisations make better decisions using data.

We work with teams across Africa to bridge the gap between data availability and data usefulness by building skills, improving processes, and embedding data into everyday operations.

Why HDD Exists

Many organisations have access to data but struggle to use it effectively. Dashboards exist, reports are generated, yet decisions are still driven by instinct and experience alone.

HDD exists to change that by helping organisations move from reactive reporting to confident, structured, data-driven decision making.

Our Approach to Data Capability

We believe data capability is not about tools alone. It is about people, process, and clarity.

Our approach starts with understanding how data is currently used, identifying gaps in skills and workflows, and then designing practical solutions that work within existing systems and realities.

We focus on sustainable capability, ensuring teams can continue to grow and operate independently long after our engagement.

What Makes Us Different

We do not believe in one-size-fits-all data solutions. Every organisation is different, and so is its data journey.

HDD combines technical expertise with a deep understanding of business context, especially within African organisations, allowing us to design solutions that are realistic, scalable, and impactful.

Our Values

Clarity, practicality, and impact guide everything we do.

We value transparency in how data is used, simplicity in how it is communicated, and integrity in how decisions are made.

Above all, we believe data should empower people, not intimidate them.

Leadership & Team

Hennotes Data Dossier is led by data professionals with experience across analytics, business intelligence, and organisational data transformation.

Our team combines technical skill with a passion for teaching, mentorship, and building long-term data capability within organisations.
